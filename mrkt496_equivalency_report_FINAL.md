- **Level:** Upper-Division (requires MRKT 396 with C or better)
- **Modality:** Available via Ecampus

**Inferred Equivalency Requirements:**
- A viable transfer candidate must be worth at least 3 semester credits (equivalent to 4.5 quarter credits, covering the 4-credit OSU requirement).
- The course must demonstrate hands-on application (a "practicum" or significant term project).
- It must cover primary data (survey design, interviewing, observational research) rather than solely focusing on secondary data analytics.

**Key Risks/Unknowns:**
- OSU may be highly protective of its "practicum" designation. A standard "Intro to Marketing Research" course might be mapped to the prerequisite (MRKT 396) rather than 496 if the syllabus does not explicitly show a heavy primary-data project.

## 5. Top Strong Petition Candidates

1. **UC Berkeley Extension - BUS ADM X460.6 (Online)**
   - **Why it is strong:** The historical syllabus structure explicitly lists observational methods, survey methods, questionnaire design, and a capstone team-based research project. This hits OSU's "practical experience in primary data" requirement perfectly.
   - **Missing Evidence:** We need the 2026 syllabus to confirm the project is still team-based and primary-data focused.
   - **Timing:** Offered "Live Online" in sequential cohorts (usually a May-July term and a Fall term), allowing plenty of time for completion and transcript delivery before Spring 2027.

2. **Arizona State University - MKT 352 (In-Person/Online, Phoenix Drivable)**
   - **Why it is strong:** This is the flagship marketing research course for ASU's heavily respected W.P. Carey School of Business. It covers research design and problem formulation at the 300-level.
   - **Missing Evidence:** A public PDF syllabus is locked behind ASU's Canvas instance. You will need to contact an ASU marketing advisor or professor for a recent syllabus to prove the "practicum" or primary data collection volume.
   - **Timing:** Guaranteed to run in Fall 2026, and typically offered in Summer 2026 as well, making it highly realistic.

3. **University of Arizona - MKTG 440 (In-Person, Tucson)**
   - **Why it is strong:** It is a 400-level course (matching OSU's 496 level) dedicated to obtaining information to understand customers. The 400-level numbering may help convince OSU evaluators that this is the advanced practicum, not just the 300-level intro.
   - **Missing Evidence:** Needs the Fall 2026 syllabus to prove the hands-on project requirement.
   - **Timing:** Fall 2026 offering is virtually guaranteed for Eller students.
